Hyderabad Dum Biryani

Description

Hyderabadi biryani, also known as Hyderabadi dum biryani, is a style of biryani from Hyderabad, India made with basmati rice and goat meat and cooked with the dum pukht method. Originating in the kitchens of the Nizam of Hyderabad, it combines elements of Hyderabadi and Mughlai cuisines. Hyderabad biryani is a key dish in Hyderabadi cuisine.

The exact origin of the dish is uncertain. Despite legends attributing it to the Nizam's chef, the biryani is of South Indian origin, derived from pilaf varieties brought to South Asia by Arab traders. Pulao may have been an army dish in medieval India. Armies would prepare a one-pot dish of rice with whichever meat was available. The distinction between "pulao" and "biryani" is arbitrary.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg meat
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp red chilli paste
  • 1 tbsp green chilli paste
  • sauteed brown onions
  • 1/2 tbsp cardamom powder
  • 3-4 cinnamon sticks
  • 1 tbsp cumin seeds
  • 4 cloves
  • A pinch of mace
  • Mint leaves
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 250 gms curd
  • 4 tbsp clarified butter
  • 750 gms semi cooked rice
  • 1 tbsp saffron
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Boiled eggs
  • Sliced carrots
  • Sliced cucumbers

Steps

  1. Clean the meat. Now in a pan add meat, salt, ginger garlic paste, red chilli powder, green chilli paste, sauteed brown onions, cardamom powder, cinnamon, cumin seeds, cloves, mace, mint leaves and lemon juice.
  2. Mix all of these ingredients thoroughly together.
  3. Add curd, clarified butter, semi cooked rice, saffron, water and oil. Mix it well.
  4. Now apply sticky dough on the sides of the pan.
  5. Cover with lid to seal it and cook for about 25 minutes.
  6. Hyderabadi Biryani is ready to eat. Garnish it with boiled eggs, sliced carrots, cucumbers and serve hot.
